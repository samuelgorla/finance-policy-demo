Corporate Finance Policy

Sample Document. Version 3.2. Effective January 1, 2026. Owner: Corporate Controller.

NOTICE: This is a sample document for Microsoft Copilot Studio demonstration purposes only.

1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes financial controls, approval thresholds, and operational procedures for all employees, contractors, and authorized representatives. It applies to expense reimbursement, purchase requisitions, vendor onboarding, and month-end close activities across all business units and global entities. Compliance is mandatory and audited quarterly by Internal Audit.

2. Expense Reimbursement Policy

2.1 Submission Requirements

All expense reports must be submitted within 30 calendar days of the expense being incurred. Reports submitted after 60 days require VP-level approval and will not be reimbursed after 90 days. Receipts are required for any single expense of $25 USD or more.

2.2 Spending Limits and Per Diems

Hotel per night: Domestic $225, International $325, approval required above limit: Manager.

Meals per day: Domestic $75, International $100, approval required above limit: Manager.

Client entertainment: Domestic $150 per attendee, International $200 per attendee, approval required: Director.

Airfare domestic: Economy required. Director approval for premium.

Airfare international over 6 hours: Premium Economy standard. VP approval for Business class.

Conference and training: Domestic $2,500, International $3,500, approval required: Director.

2.3 Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are NOT reimbursable: Personal entertainment unrelated to client business. Alcohol exceeding 20 percent of any single meal receipt. Spa, gym, or recreational facility charges. Traffic violations and parking fines. Personal travel insurance. Hotel mini-bar charges and in-room movies.

Reimbursement is processed via direct deposit within 10 business days of approval. Contact ap-support@example.com for inquiries beyond 14 days.

3. Purchase Order Process

3.1 When a PO is Required

A purchase order is required for all purchases of $1,000 USD or greater, all recurring service contracts regardless of value, and any engagement with a new vendor. Purchases under $1,000 may be made via corporate credit card with manager approval.

3.2 Approval Thresholds

$0 to $4,999: Cost Center Manager approval required.

$5,000 to $24,999: Manager plus Director approval required.

$25,000 to $99,999: Director plus VP approval required.

$100,000 to $499,999: VP plus CFO approval required.

$500,000 and above: CFO plus CEO plus Board notification required.

3.3 Submission Steps

1. Confirm budget availability with your cost center manager.

2. Create a requisition in the procurement system with a clear business justification.

3. Attach vendor quote no more than 90 days old and statement of work where applicable.

4. Route for approval per Section 3.2. Routing is automatic via the procurement portal.

5. Once approved, the system issues a PO number. Do not authorize work without a PO.

6. Receive goods or services, then mark the PO as received within 5 business days.

Note: Splitting a single purchase into multiple smaller POs to avoid approval thresholds is a policy violation and may result in disciplinary action.

4. New Vendor Onboarding

New vendors must complete onboarding before any PO can be issued. The process typically takes 5 to 7 business days and requires: W-9 for US vendors or W-8BEN for non-US vendors. Bank verification letter or voided check. Certificate of Insurance if vendor performs on-site work. Master Services Agreement if engagement exceeds $50,000 or involves data access. Anti-bribery and Code of Conduct attestation.

Submit all documents to vendor-onboarding@example.com. Track status via the Procurement Portal.

5. Month-End Close Calendar

AP cutoff for invoices: Last business day at 3:00 PM. Owner: AP Team.

Expense report cutoff: Last business day at 5:00 PM. Owner: All employees.

Bank reconciliation: Business Day plus 2. Owner: Treasury.

Accruals submitted: Business Day plus 3. Owner: Cost Center Owners.

Trial balance review: Business Day plus 4. Owner: Controller.

Variance commentary: Business Day plus 5. Owner: FP&A.

Close locked in ERP: Business Day plus 6. Owner: Controller.

Management reporting pack: Business Day plus 8. Owner: FP&A.

6. Approval Delegations

Approvers unavailable for more than 3 consecutive business days must set up a formal delegation in the approval workflow system. Delegates must be at the same level or higher. Configure via My Profile then Delegation Setup in the Workflow Settings portal.

7. Corporate Credit Card Usage

Monthly limits: $5,000 for individual contributors, $15,000 for managers and above. Limit increases require Director approval.

Personal purchases on corporate cards are strictly prohibited. Cards must be reconciled monthly. Unreconciled charges after 45 days result in payroll deduction. Lost cards must be reported within 24 hours to card-services@example.com. Cards must be returned within 5 business days of separation.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who approves my expense report? A: Your direct manager approves by default. Amounts exceeding category limits require an additional approver per Section 2.2.

Q: How do I add a new vendor? A: Email vendor-onboarding@example.com with W-9 or W-8BEN, bank verification, and Certificate of Insurance. Onboarding completes in 5 to 7 business days.

Q: Can I book business class for an international flight? A: Business class requires VP approval and is only permitted for flights longer than 6 hours. Premium Economy is the default for qualifying international flights.

Q: What happens if I miss the expense submission deadline? A: Reports 30 to 60 days old require manager approval. Reports 60 to 90 days old require VP approval. Reports older than 90 days are generally not reimbursed.

Q: Can I split a large purchase into smaller POs? A: No. Splitting purchases to avoid approval thresholds is a policy violation and may result in disciplinary action.

Q: Who do I contact for finance policy questions? A: Policy interpretation: finance-policy@example.com. Expense issues: ap-support@example.com. PO questions: procurement@example.com.
